Purplebricks vs Local Estate Agents: The Ultimate 2024 Data Comparison for UK Sellers
Purplebricks vs Local Estate Agents Comparison: A modern workspace featuring a laptop with data charts, a tablet displaying a purple house icon, and a traditional estate agent brochure, symbolizing the data-driven choice between online and high-street agents.



Selling your home is likely the single largest financial transaction you will ever undertake. In the UK property market, the "traditional" way of doing things—walking into a high-street branch and shaking hands with a local agent—has been disrupted over the last decade by the rise of online and hybrid models, most notably Purplebricks.

The debate between Purplebricks and local estate agents is often framed as a simple choice between "cheap and digital" vs "expensive and personal." However, when you dig into the data, the reality is far more nuanced.

Is the upfront saving of an online agent worth the potential loss in final sale price? Does a local agent's "boots on the ground" presence actually result in a faster sale? In this comprehensive data-driven guide, we break down everything UK homeowners need to know to make an informed decision.

1. The Fundamental Difference in Business Models
To understand the data, we first need to understand what you are actually paying for.

The Purplebricks "Hybrid" Model
Purplebricks operates as a hybrid agency. They don’t have high-street branches, which significantly reduces their overheads. Instead, they employ Local Property Experts (LPEs) who cover specific postcodes.

Payment Structure: You typically pay a fixed fee upfront (or via a deferred payment agreement), regardless of whether your property sells.
Technology: The process is heavily managed via an app or online portal, where sellers can track viewings, feedback, and offers.
The Traditional Local Estate Agent Model
Local agents (like Savills, Knight Frank, or independent local shops) operate from physical branches.

Payment Structure: They usually work on a "no sale, no fee" basis. You pay a percentage commission (typically 1% to 2% + VAT) only upon successful completion.
Personal Service: You have a dedicated team in an office that you can visit. They handle everything from key holding and viewings to intensive sales progression.

The "Upfront Fee" Risk
The data suggests that while Purplebricks is cheaper on paper, it carries a unique risk: The Sunk Cost. If your home doesn't sell with Purplebricks, you have already spent £1,000+. With a local agent, if they fail to find a buyer, you owe them nothing.

According to various industry audits, local agents are incentivised to push for the highest possible price because their fee is tied to it. An online agent has already collected their fee (or is guaranteed to collect it via the payment agreement), which some critics argue reduces the urgency to negotiate the final few thousand pounds of a sale.

3. Sales Performance Data: Who Sells Faster?
Data from property portals like Rightmove and Zoopla provides a clear picture of how these models perform in terms of speed and success rates.

Time to Secure a Buyer
Recent market analysis indicates that high-street agents tend to secure buyers slightly faster in complex or "cool" markets. In a "hot" seller’s market (where demand vastly outstrips supply), the difference is negligible—properties sell themselves.

The Conversion Gap
Industry data from The Advisory suggests that high-street agents have a higher "instruction-to-sale" conversion rate. This is often attributed to Sales Progression. A sale isn't finished when an offer is accepted; it's finished when contracts are exchanged. Local agents often spend 50% of their time chasing solicitors and managing chains. In the online model, much of this burden falls on the seller or a centralised (often non-local) support team.

4. Achieving the Asking Price: The 5% Difference
This is the most critical data point for any homeowner. If a local agent charges £2,000 more in fees but achieves a sale price that is 2% higher, they have effectively paid for themselves and put more money in your pocket.

Negotiating Power
A study by Which? and various independent property consultants has shown that experienced local negotiators often achieve between 0.5% and 3% more on the final sale price compared to DIY or hybrid models.

Example Scenario (Property Value £300,000):

Purplebricks: Fee £1,199. Sale price achieved: £295,000. Net: £293,801.
Local Agent: Fee 1.5% (£4,500). Sale price achieved: £300,000. Net: £295,500.
In this data-backed example, the "expensive" agent actually made the seller £1,699 more profit than the "cheap" online option.

5. Service Levels: What’s Included?
A common frustration for Purplebricks users is the "Add-on" culture. To get a service comparable to a local agent, you often have to pay extra.

The Accompanied Viewings Data
Local Agents: Almost always include accompanied viewings in their percentage fee. They hold the keys and show the house while you are at work.
Purplebricks: The base fee often requires the seller to conduct their own viewings. To have a Purplebricks LPE conduct viewings, there is usually an additional charge (often around £300-£500).
Local Knowledge & Valuation Accuracy
Data shows that Purplebricks LPEs often cover much larger territories than high-street agents.

A high-street agent might only cover a 3-mile radius. They know which side of the street is more desirable and which school catchment areas are shifting.
A hybrid LPE might cover an entire city or multiple postcodes. This can lead to "algorithm-heavy" valuations that don't account for the micro-nuances of a specific street, potentially leading to over-pricing (no viewings) or under-pricing (lost money).
6. Transparency and Customer Reviews
When looking at volume, Purplebricks has thousands of five-star reviews on Trustpilot. However, it is essential to look at when those reviews are requested.

Online Models: Often ask for a review immediately after the "Instruction" phase (when the LPE has visited and the photos look great).
Local Agents: Reviews on platforms like allAgents are typically left after completion.
7. The Pros and Cons Summary
Purplebricks
Pros:

Significant upfront cost savings.
Excellent 24/7 tech platform for viewing management.
Professional photography and floorplans included.
Ideal for "easy-to-sell" homes in high-demand areas.
Cons:

Fee is payable even if the property doesn't sell.
Sellers often have to do their own viewings.
Less intensive sales progression support.
LPEs are often stretched across large territories.
Local Estate Agents
Pros:

No sale, no fee—zero financial risk to the seller.
Stronger incentive to achieve the highest possible price.
In-depth knowledge of local micro-markets.
Comprehensive sales progression (managing the chain).
Accompanied viewings included as standard.
Cons:

Higher commission fees upon completion.
May lack the 24/7 digital interface of online rivals.
Service quality can vary significantly between different branches of the same brand.
8. Final Verdict: Which One Should You Choose?
The data leads us to a clear conclusion: Your choice should depend on your property and your personality.

Choose Purplebricks if:
You are in a "Hot" Market: If houses on your street sell within 48 hours, you don't need a high-end negotiator.
You are Price-Sensitive Upfront: You have the cash ready and want to cap your costs.
You are Tech-Savvy: You prefer managing your own calendar and talking directly to buyers via an app.
You are a Confident Presenter: You are happy to show people around your home yourself.
Choose a Local Estate Agent if:
Your Property is Unique or High-Value: Premium homes require a bespoke marketing strategy and a "black book" of wealthy buyers.
You are in a Complex Chain: If you are buying and selling simultaneously, you need a professional to manage the solicitors and other agents in the chain.
You Want the Best Price, Not the Lowest Fee: You recognise that a skilled negotiator can add more to the sale price than they charge in commission.
You are Busy: You want to hand over the keys and only be bothered when a serious offer is on the table.
Conclusion
The "Purplebricks vs Local Estate Agents" debate isn't about which is "better," but which is better for you.

Data proves that while Purplebricks offers an unbeatable entry price, local agents often provide a more secure path to a successful completion at a higher price point. For most UK sellers, the peace of mind offered by a "No Sale, No Fee" agreement and local expertise outweighs the initial digital saving.

Before you instruct any agent, we recommend getting three valuations: one from an online agent like Purplebricks and two from reputable local high-street firms. Compare their valuations, their local success data, and their rapport. Only then will you have the data you need to sell your biggest asset with confidence.
