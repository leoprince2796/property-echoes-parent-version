Purplebricks vs Yopa: Which Online Estate Agent is Best for Your Home Sale?
Selling your home is one of the most significant financial decisions you will make. In recent years, the UK property market has seen a seismic shift, with digital-first estate agents like Purplebricks and Yopa promising to disrupt the traditional high-street model.

By cutting out the physical shopfront and leveraging technology, these platforms offer lower upfront costs and 24/7 service. However, deciding between the two market leaders requires more than just comparing their headline fees.

In this guide, we break down the service models, pricing structures, and customer experiences of Purplebricks and Yopa to help you decide which agent is right for your property sale.

The Rise of Online Estate Agency in the UK
The traditional model of paying a commission fee—often 1% to 2.5% of your final sale price—is no longer the only option for UK homeowners. Online agents have introduced a "hybrid" model, combining digital platform efficiency with local property experts to handle valuations, negotiations, and sales progression.

Both Purplebricks and Yopa have become household names, but they operate with distinct differences that can impact the speed and success of your sale.

1. Purplebricks: The Market Pioneer
As the most recognised brand in the online agency space, Purplebricks has revolutionised the market. Their approach is built on their proprietary digital portal, which keeps homeowners in the loop at every stage of the sale.

How Purplebricks Works
When you choose Purplebricks, you are assigned a Local Property Expert (LPE). They visit your home, conduct the valuation, and oversee the creation of your listing. Once live, the "Hub" app becomes your command centre.

Pros:
Brand Dominance: Massive brand awareness means high traffic to their listings.
The Hub App: Exceptionally intuitive for tracking viewing requests and feedback.
Flexible Options: Various packages to suit different budgets, including options to pay upfront or defer payment.
Cons:
Mixed Reviews on Support: Because they handle such a high volume of properties, some users report variable levels of personal service.
Viewing Logistics: In basic packages, the onus is on the seller to conduct viewings.
2. Yopa: The Consistent Challenger
Yopa has built its reputation on consistency and a more "traditional" feel despite being an online agency. Many sellers find Yopa’s structure feels more familiar if they are used to high-street agents.

How Yopa Works
Similar to Purplebricks, you get a dedicated local agent. Yopa prides itself on their "No Sale, No Fee" options and a highly streamlined sales progression team that stays involved until completion.

Pros:
High Trustpilot Ratings: Generally known for very consistent customer support.
Dedicated Progression Team: Excellent communication during the stressful period between offer acceptance and exchange of contracts.
Clear Pricing: Transparent fee structure with no hidden surprises.
Cons:
Service Costs: Depending on the package and add-ons (like professional photography or floorplans), costs can occasionally climb closer to budget high-street levels.
Regional Variation: As with all agents, the quality of your specific local agent is crucial to your experience.
Critical Factors for Choosing Your Agent
When deciding between Purplebricks and Yopa, consider these three pillars of a successful home sale:

A. The Importance of Viewings
Are you comfortable showing strangers around your home yourself?

Seller-led viewings are the cheapest option.
Accompanied viewings (conducted by the agent) usually cost extra. Check both agents' current packages for "Accompanied Viewings" add-ons if you prefer a hands-off approach.
B. Pricing Transparency
Always ask for the "total" price. Both companies often advertise a low headline fee, but once you add premium listings, featured placements on Rightmove, and accompanied viewings, the price can change. Always get a written quote.

C. Local Expertise
An agent’s ability to sell your home depends on their knowledge of your specific street, neighbourhood, and buyer profile. During your free valuation, ask the agent:

How many homes have you sold on this street in the last 12 months?
What is your strategy for pricing my home to attract maximum interest?
Final Verdict: Which is Best?
There is no single "best" online estate agent. The right choice depends on your property and your priorities:

Choose Purplebricks if: You are tech-savvy, want the most robust digital app experience, and are comfortable managing parts of the sale process yourself to save on fees.
Choose Yopa if: You prioritise consistent communication, want a dedicated team that stays closely involved throughout the sales progression, and prefer a service that feels slightly more like a traditional high-street experience.
