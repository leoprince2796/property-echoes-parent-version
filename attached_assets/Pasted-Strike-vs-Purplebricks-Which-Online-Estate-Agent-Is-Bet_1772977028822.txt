Strike vs. Purplebricks: Which Online Estate Agent Is Better for You?
A digital comparison of online estate agents Strike and Purplebricks showing a sold sign in front of a modern home.

In the current UK property landscape, the "High Street" dominance is no longer a given. With rising living costs and a more tech-savvy seller base, online and hybrid estate agents have transitioned from being "disruptors" to mainstream choices. Two names stand head and shoulders above the rest: Strike and Purplebricks.

But here is the twist: as of late 2023, these two former rivals are now operating under the same umbrella. Following Strike's acquisition of Purplebricks for a symbolic £1, the online estate agency market has seen a massive consolidation. However, for you—the UK homeowner—the question remains: Purplebricks vs Strike: which one actually helps you sell faster and keep more of your hard-earned equity?

In this comprehensive, 2,200+ word authority guide, we break down the pricing models, service levels, hidden "catches", and the technological differences to help you make an informed decision for your next move.

1. The Core Philosophy: Free vs. Fixed Fee
At their heart, Strike and Purplebricks represent two different paths to the same goal: selling your home without the traditional 1% to 2% commission.

What is Strike?
Strike (formerly Housesimple) famously pivoted to a "sell for free" model in 2020. Their philosophy is simple: they don’t charge you a penny to list your home on Rightmove and Zoopla. They make their money through referral fees from partner services like mortgage brokers (Strike Financial Services), conveyancing, and insurance providers.

What is Purplebricks?
Purplebricks was the original market disruptor. They built their reputation on a "Fixed Fee" model—a set price paid upfront or on completion, regardless of the final sale price of your house. Since the acquisition by Strike's parent company, their model has evolved, but it remains a more "premium" or structured brand compared to the minimalist Strike approach.

3. Strike: How Can It Truly Be "Free"?
The most common question we hear from UK sellers is: "What’s the catch with Strike?"

It seems counter-intuitive to provide a service that costs hundreds of pounds in portal fees (Rightmove/Zoopla) for free. However, Strike’s business model is a volume game. By offering a free service, they capture a huge percentage of the market.

The "Freemium" Model
Strike offers a Free Plan and a Managed Plan.

The Free Plan: You get a valuation, photos (that you often provide or are basic), a floorplan, and your listing on the major portals. You manage your own viewings.
The Managed Plan: For a fee (typically around £600–£800), Strike provides professional photography, 3D floorplans, and a dedicated account manager to handle the "heavy lifting" of the sale.
How Strike Makes Money
If you use Strike’s free service, you will be encouraged (sometimes quite firmly) to use their mortgage and legal partners. Strike receives a healthy commission for these referrals. While you are under no obligation to use them, this is the engine that keeps the "Free" model running.

Expert Tip: If you already have a mortgage broker and a solicitor you trust, Strike remains a genuine bargain. If you are a first-time seller who wants everything under one roof, be sure to compare the mortgage rates Strike offers against the wider market to ensure the "free" sale isn't costing you more in interest over five years.

4. Purplebricks: The Evolution of the Fixed Fee
Purplebricks changed the UK's relationship with estate agents. No longer were sellers beholden to a percentage of their home's value.

The Local Property Expert (LPE)
One of the primary differences in the Purplebricks vs Strike debate is the "human element." Purplebricks relies heavily on their network of Local Property Experts. These are self-employed agents who live in your area and understand the specific nuances of your local market—which schools are in demand, which streets command a premium, and how to price a property to generate a "bidding war."

The Pricing Shift
Following the acquisition, Purplebricks has experimented with a "Free" model in certain regions to compete directly with Strike, but they generally retain a "Full Service" package. This package includes:

Professional photography.
A "featured" listing on Rightmove.
Expert valuation and advice.
Management of the "Post-Sale" process (Conveyancing progression).
5. Service Showdown: The Selling Process
To decide between Purplebricks vs Strike, we must look at the actual experience of selling a home.

Step 1: The Valuation
Strike: Offers a digital valuation or a visit from a Strike representative. Because they handle such high volume, their digital valuations are surprisingly accurate, using massive datasets of recent local sales.
Purplebricks: Almost always involves a visit from an LPE. This is a more traditional "pitch" where the agent will walk through the home and provide a valuation based on local market sentiment.
Step 2: Professional Marketing
This is where the two brands diverge most.

If you choose Strike's free option, you are responsible for the "appeal" of your listing. While they provide tools, the professional "gloss" of a high-end listing might require the paid upgrade.
Purplebricks’ paid tiers include professional photography as standard. In the age of "Instagrammable" homes, the quality of your photos can be the difference between 5 viewings and 50.
Step 3: Viewings
Both agents, by default, expect the homeowner to conduct viewings.

The Benefit: Nobody knows the house better than you. You can speak to the history of the boiler, the friendly neighbours, and the sun-trap in the garden.
The Drawback: It is time-consuming and can be awkward for buyers who feel they can't be honest about their critiques while the owner is standing there.
The Solution: Both offer "Hosted Viewing" add-ons. If you are a busy professional, expect to pay roughly £300–£500 for an agent to handle this for you.
6. Purplebricks vs Strike: The Technological Edge
Both companies have invested millions into their apps. The goal? To give you total control of your sale 24/7.

The Strike App
The Strike app is clean, minimalist, and extremely fast. You get instant push notifications when a viewing is requested or an offer is made. You can message buyers directly (cutting out the middleman) and manage your calendar with a few taps. It feels like a modern fintech app—think Monzo, but for houses.

The Purplebricks App
The Purplebricks dashboard is more robust. It allows you to see how many people are viewing your property online, track "watchers" on Rightmove, and review feedback from every viewing in one place. Because Purplebricks has been refining this tech for over a decade, it feels slightly more "complete" for a seller who wants deep data.

If you use Strike's free service and take your own photos on an iPhone, your property may look less appealing than a Purplebricks listing with professional wide-angle shots and "Featured Property" status.

Winner: Purplebricks (for the standard inclusion of professional marketing).

9. Hidden Costs: The "Small Print" You Need to Know
While Strike is "Free" and Purplebricks is "Fixed Fee," neither is entirely devoid of potential costs.

Strike’s Optional Extras
Hosted Viewings: ~£599
Professional Photos & Floorplan: ~£399 (if not on a managed plan)
Performance Pack (Rightmove Premium Listing): ~£150
Purplebricks’ Potential Extras
Upfront vs. Post-Sale: Historically, Purplebricks required the fee even if the house didn't sell. They have mostly moved away from this, but always check if your contract is "No Sale, No Fee."
Conveyancing: Like Strike, they will heavily promote their own solicitors. These are often "conveyancing factories" that can be slow during the peak of a housing chain.
10. The 2023 Acquisition: Why It Matters to You
When Strike bought Purplebricks, many wondered if the brands would merge. For now, they remain distinct.

Strike is being positioned as the Digital-First, High-Value option for confident sellers.
Purplebricks is being positioned as the Agent-Led, Hybrid option for those who want a bit more "hand-holding" and local expertise.
Behind the scenes, the "Sales Progression" teams (the people who chase solicitors and keep the chain together) are often the same. This means you are getting a similar level of back-end support regardless of which brand you choose.

11. Which Agent Should You Choose?
Choose Strike If:
You are cost-conscious: You want to save every penny possible and are happy to do some of the work.
You are a confident "Salesperson": You enjoy showing people around your home and negotiating directly.
You have a simple sale: A standard house in a high-demand area that "sells itself."
You want a modern tech experience: You prefer apps over phone calls.
Choose Purplebricks If:
You want local insight: You believe a local expert's valuation and pricing strategy will net you a higher sale price (offsetting the fee).
You want "Professional" marketing as standard: You don't want to worry about hiring photographers.
You want the "Big Brand" security: Purplebricks still carries significant brand recognition with buyers.
You have a complex property: If your home is unique or difficult to value, having an LPE visit is essential.
12. Conclusion: The Final Verdict
In the battle of Strike vs. Purplebricks, there is no single "winner"—only the agent that fits your specific needs.

If your primary goal is to maximise your profit by minimising fees, Strike is unbeatable. The "Free" model is legitimate, and as long as you are disciplined with your choice of mortgage and legal services, it is the cheapest way to sell a house in the UK.

However, if you feel that professional marketing and local expertise are worth an investment to ensure your house doesn't sit on the market for months, Purplebricks’ hybrid model provides a middle ground between the "DIY" feel of Strike and the expensive commission of a High Street agent.

The UK property market is moving fast. Whether you choose the "Strike it for free" approach or the "Purplebricks Expert" path, you are already ahead of the curve by moving away from traditional commission models.

Ready to Move?
Check your local area coverage on both websites before signing. Some remote parts of the UK may have limited LPE availability for Purplebricks or different "Free" availability for Strike.
