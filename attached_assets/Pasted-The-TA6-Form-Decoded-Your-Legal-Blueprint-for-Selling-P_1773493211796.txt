The TA6 Form Decoded: Your Legal Blueprint for Selling Property
Selling a property in the UK is a complex legal journey. Among the sea of paperwork, one document stands as the cornerstone of the entire process: the Property Information Form (TA6).

Whether you are a first-time seller or a seasoned homeowner, understanding what the TA6 form is and how to complete it accurately is non-negotiable. Mistakes here don’t just cause delays; they can lead to legal disputes, price renegotiations, or the total collapse of your sale.

In this guide, we decode the TA6, explain why it matters, and provide the blueprint you need to navigate it with confidence.

What is a Property Information Form TA6?
The TA6 Property Information Form is an industry-standard document created by the Law Society of England and Wales. It is designed to be completed by the seller and provides the buyer with detailed information about the property, its history, and its current condition.

Think of the TA6 as the "disclosure document" for your home. It covers everything from boundaries and planning permission to disputes with neighbours and environmental hazards.

Why is the TA6 Form Essential?
Transparency: It allows the buyer to make an informed decision based on facts, not assumptions.
Legal Liability: The information provided on the form forms part of the contract. If you provide false information or deliberately omit critical facts, you could be sued for misrepresentation.
Speeding Up the Transaction: By providing comprehensive information upfront, you reduce the number of enquiries raised by the buyer’s solicitor, smoothing the path to exchange.
What Information Does the TA6 Form Cover?
The TA6 is comprehensive. While it is regularly updated (with the 6th edition being the current standard), it typically requires you to provide factual answers to questions regarding:

Section	What you need to disclose
Boundaries	Who maintains which fences, walls, and hedges?
Disputes	Are there ongoing arguments with neighbours?
Planning & Building	Have you had any extensions or structural changes? Were regulations followed?
Guarantees	Do you have warranties for damp-proofing, roof repairs, or windows?
Services	How is the property heated? Are there any issues with drainage or utility supply?
Environmental	Has the property ever been flooded? Is there evidence of Japanese Knotweed?
Your Legal Blueprint: How to Complete the TA6 Accurately
Completing this form can feel daunting. Here is your step-by-step approach to ensuring you remain legally protected.

1. Be Honest, Be Detailed, Be Accurate
The most golden rule: Do not guess. If you do not know the answer to a question, state "not known." If you are unsure whether something should be declared, talk to your solicitor. It is far better to disclose a minor issue upfront than to have it discovered during a survey.

2. Gather Your Documents Before You Start
Don't wait until you sit down to fill out the form. Start a "Property File" today. Collect:

Planning permission approvals.
Building Regulations completion certificates.
Fensa certificates for windows.
Gas and electrical safety certificates.
Any guarantees for home improvements.
3. Disclose Known Defects
You are not required to repair everything, but you are required to disclose what you know. If you are aware of a leaking roof or a boundary dispute, be transparent. Failing to do so is a common cause of post-completion legal action.

Common Pitfalls: What Sellers Get Wrong
Even well-intentioned sellers make mistakes. Here are the most common "red flags" that lead to problems:

"Forgetting" Past Disputes: You might think a argument with your neighbour three years ago is irrelevant. If it was formal or involved authorities, it must be disclosed.
Unauthorised Building Work: You built an extension but didn't get Building Regulations approval? Disclose it. Your solicitor may suggest getting "Indemnity Insurance" to cover this, which can save the sale.
Japanese Knotweed: The TA6 explicitly asks about this. Never ignore it. If there is, or was, Knotweed on the property, you must say so.
The Buyer's Perspective: Why You Should Read the TA6 Closely
If you are a buyer, do not treat the TA6 as a tick-box exercise. It is your most valuable window into the reality of the home you are purchasing.

Questions to ask your solicitor based on the TA6:

"Does the TA6 show any planning permissions that were applied for but refused?"
"Are there any restrictive covenants mentioned that could stop me from putting up a fence or conservatory?"
"Does the seller mention any recurring maintenance issues with the boiler or roof?"
Frequently Asked Questions
Is the TA6 form legally binding?
Yes. The information you provide is relied upon by the buyer. If you give incorrect information, you may be held liable for losses the buyer suffers as a result.

What if I don't know the answer?
Always tick "Not known" rather than guessing. If you guess and are wrong, you could be held liable.

Does the TA6 replace a survey?
Absolutely not. The TA6 provides the seller's knowledge. A professional surveyor provides an expert, independent assessment of the property's physical condition. You need both.

Final Thoughts: A Proactive Approach Saves Money
The TA6 form is not just administrative paperwork; it is a vital tool for a successful transaction. By treating it as a serious legal document and taking the time to fill it out accurately with the help of your solicitor, you protect yourself from future litigation and create trust with your buyer.
